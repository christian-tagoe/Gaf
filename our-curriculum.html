Our Curriculum

We have a three stage curriculum over 12 months which covers the foundational, intermediate, and advanced levels of carpentry skills. This curriculum is designed to progressively build the skills of carpentry students, starting from the basics and advancing to specialized areas. It provides a comprehensive foundation for students to either enter the workforce or pursue entrepreneurship in the carpentry industry. Additionally, each stage includes hands-on projects to ensure practical application of the learned skills.

learn more

Stage 1: Foundation Carpentry Skills (Beginner Level)

Weeks 1-4: Introduction to Carpentry

Overview of carpentry as a trade, its history, and its significance in construction.

Basic safety procedures and proper tool handling.

Introduction to different types of wood and their characteristics.

Understanding and reading basic carpentry blueprints and plans.

Gas heating

Central heating

Smartphone solutions

Weeks 5-8: Hand Tools and Basic Joinery Technique

Identification and proper use of hand tools (hammers, saws, chisels, etc.).

Hands-on exercises for mastering basic joinery techniques, including butt joints, lap joints, and miter joints.

Introduction to measuring and marking techniques.

Simple carpentry projects to apply learned skills.

Weeks 9-12: Introduction to Power Tools

Safety protocols for using power tools.

Hands-on training with power saws, drills, and sanders.

Basic understanding of wood finishes and surface preparation.

Building small projects using power tools.

Stage 2: Intermediate carpentry skills

Weeks 13-16: Advanced Joinery and Furniture Making

In-depth study of advanced joinery techniques such as dovetail joints and mortise and tenon joints.

Introduction to furniture design principles.

Building small furniture pieces like tables and chairs.

Weeks 17-20: Cabinetry and Built-In Furniture

Understanding cabinet design and construction.

Installation techniques for built-in furniture.

Advanced woodworking skills for creating intricate designs.

Weeks 21-24: Carpentry Project Management

Introduction to project management concepts.

Planning and executing carpentry projects, including estimating materials and labour.

Budgeting and cost control in carpentry projects.

Stage 3: Advanced carpentry specializations

Weeks 25-28: Advanced Finishing Techniques

Mastery of advanced finishing techniques, including staining, varnishing, and lacquering.

Hands-on experience with different finishes on various wood types.

Restoration techniques for antique furniture.

Weeks 29-32: Green Building and Sustainable Carpentry

Understanding eco-friendly and sustainable carpentry practices.

Incorporating recycled materials and energy-efficient designs.

Certification in sustainable carpentry practices.

Weeks 33-36: Entrepreneurship and Business Skills

Basics of starting and managing a carpentry business.

Marketing and branding for carpenters.

Legal and financial aspects of running a carpentry business.
